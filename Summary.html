1. ADC (Analog-to-Digital Converter)
ADC converts continuous analog neural signals into discrete digital values.
  • Higher ADC resolution improves waveform accuracy.
  • However, it increases data volume and communication cost.
  • In this study, ADC resolutions of 4, 8, 12, and 16 bits were compared.
2. Difference Between RMSE and Correlation Coefficient

RMSE→ Measures the magnitude of error from the original waveform

Correlation Coefficient→ Measures the similarity of waveform shape

Example
  • Small RMSE → waveform values are close to the original.
  • High correlation coefficient → waveform features and trends are preserved.

A waveform can have a high correlation coefficient but a large RMSE if the overall shape is similar but the amplitude is different.
